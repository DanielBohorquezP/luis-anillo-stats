Why it matters

Biostatistics and epidemiological analysis determine whether your study gets published or rejected.

In medical and clinical research, good data poorly analyzed is worthless. High-impact journals demand correct methods: the right design, the appropriate statistical test, well-specified models, and rigorously interpreted results.

Health statistics (or biostatistics) translates your data into evidence: it measures associations, controls for bias, models risks, and predicts outcomes. It is the difference between a returned manuscript and an accepted article in a Q1 journal.

Epidemiological analysis identifies risk factors, measures disease frequency, and evaluates the effectiveness of public health interventions. With the correct techniques — logistic regression, survival models, spatial analysis — your study will contribute solid evidence to the field.

What are the benefits of hiring a biostatistical consultant for clinical trials?

A biostatistical consultant ensures your clinical trial is correctly designed from the start — preventing methodological errors that lead to journal rejection. Key benefits include: appropriate sample size calculation for statistical power, correct hypothesis tests for your data type, well-specified regression models that control confounders, reproducible analysis in R/Python/STATA, and expert responses to statistical reviewers' comments.
